# Fair Use Policy

## Purpose

This service provides the Cambridge University community with access to large language models for research, teaching, and learning. Our goal is to maximise access for the broadest range of legitimate uses while ensuring fair resource distribution.

## Who Can Use This Service

- Researchers and academic staff at Cambridge University
- Graduate and undergraduate students for coursework and research
- Staff developing tools and services for the university community

## Acceptable Uses

- Research experiments and data analysis
- Educational projects and coursework
- Prototyping applications (including potential spin-outs)
- Integration with development tools (Cursor, Copilot, etc.)
- Agentic systems and multi-turn workflows
- Interactive use and chat interfaces

## What We Ask of You

- **Batch where possible** — if you're processing many prompts, use async requests rather than sequential calls
- **Right-size your context** — don't send 32k tokens if 4k will do
- **Be a good neighbour** — sustained heavy use during peak hours affects everyone

## Prohibited Uses

The following uses are strictly prohibited:

### Harm to people

- Harassment, threats, defamation, or intimidation
- Content promoting suicide, self-harm, or disordered eating
- Sexual violence or non-consensual intimate content
- Terrorism, hate-based violence, or weapons development
- Malicious cyber activity or intellectual property infringement

### Privacy violations

- Aggregating or profiling individuals' private information without consent
- Facial recognition databases without data subject consent
- Using someone's likeness without consent in ways that could confuse authenticity
- Social scoring or inferring sensitive attributes from personal data

### Harm to minors

- Any content that exploits, endangers, or sexualises anyone under 18
- Grooming, or exposing minors to age-inappropriate content

### Manipulation and deception

- Academic dishonesty (submitting AI output as your own work without disclosure where required by your department)
- Fraud, scams, spam, or impersonation
- Political campaigning, lobbying, or election interference

### High-stakes decisions without human oversight

- Automated decisions in employment, housing, finance, legal, medical, or other sensitive domains without appropriate human review

This list is not exhaustive. We reserve the right to revoke access for any use that violates university policy, UK law, or the spirit of this policy.

## Research Exemptions

Some prohibited uses may be permissible in a supervised research context with appropriate ethical oversight. For example:

- Studying online harms, extremism, or abuse patterns
- Security research and adversarial testing
- Analysis of misinformation or deceptive content
- Criminological or psychological research involving sensitive topics

If your research requires use cases that would otherwise be prohibited, you must:

1. Have ethics approval from your department or the university ethics committee
2. Contact us *before* beginning work to discuss your requirements
3. Document appropriate safeguards for any sensitive outputs

We reserve the right to require additional safeguards or decline requests that pose unacceptable risk.

## Monitoring

We log request metadata (timestamps, token counts, latency) per API key to ensure fair distribution and diagnose issues.

**We do not log prompt content**.

## Enforcement

We reserve the right to throttle or revoke access for:

- Sustained usage that degrades service for others
- Sharing API keys outside the university
- Any prohibited use listed above
